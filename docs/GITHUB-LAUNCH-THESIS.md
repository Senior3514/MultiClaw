# GitHub Launch Thesis

## Ambition

Build MultiClaw as a category-defining open-source product that people instantly want to star, try, share, and build on.

## What would make that possible

### 1. Instant wow
The first 30 seconds must feel different.

### 2. Clear category
People should immediately understand:
- this is not another assistant
- this is not another wrapper
- this creates a company around a product

### 3. Easy first run
Trying MultiClaw should feel simple, not exhausting.

### 4. Strong examples
The repo should make it easy to imagine real use cases.

### 5. Beautiful product feel
The project must look serious, premium, and alive.

### 6. Real utility
People star products that feel useful, not just visionary.

## Launch rule

Do not chase hype without product quality.
The product itself must earn excitement.

## Product magnets
- clean landing experience
- protected workspace flow
- clear examples
- strong screenshots and short demo clips
- docs people can actually follow
- clear install path
- obvious extensibility

## Long-term truth

If MultiClaw becomes a product people immediately want to show other builders, growth becomes much easier.
