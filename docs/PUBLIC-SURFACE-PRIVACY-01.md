# MultiClaw Public Surface Privacy 01

## Core rule

MultiClaw must not leak private details from the creator's real life, private infrastructure, or backstage working context into the public product surface.

## Keep backstage private

The following should stay out of the public-facing product by default:
- personal details
- private life context
- internal operator lore
- hidden infrastructure details
- private hostnames, usernames, or sensitive paths
- accidental references to the creator's own environment

## Product standard

The product should feel self-contained and universal.
People who discover it on GitHub or elsewhere should see a clean product, not the private story behind its creation.

## Public impression

The reaction should be:
- what is this?
- I need this now

Not:
- whose private setup is this?
- why are there internal life details here?

## Rule of execution

Backstage context can help build the product.
It should not become the product.
